S01 Examine Sea Level

The change in sea level called the tide is driven by the Moon and Sun. Water from the ocean is pulled through the Golden Gate into San Francisco Bay, resulting in a high tide with a higher than average sea level. [picture of high tide] [Map of San Francisco Bay]

About 6 hours later, the seawater moves out of San Francisco Bay and the sea level at the baylands falls to below average. The plants in the marsh that were flooded by seawater are slowing exposed as the tide falls.

Along the west coast of North America, there are two high tides and two low tides just about every day.

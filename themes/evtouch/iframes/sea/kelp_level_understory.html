Kelp Forest Understory

To describe a kelp forest, scientists use the same terms you’d hear in a discussion of tropical rain forests. The understory comes just below the thick growth of the canopy. Although the greatest concentrations of larger marine animals flourish atop the canopy and at the forest floor, creatures occupy the full length of a kelp plant. In the dim light of the understory, algae thrive in the nutrient-rich water. Billions of plankton turn the water so soupy that divers in this region may not be able to see their fins.

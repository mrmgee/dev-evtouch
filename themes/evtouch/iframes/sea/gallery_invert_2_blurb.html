Kelp Crab

Dwelling among the branches of the kelp holdfast—the root system that holds the kelp to the bottom—kelp crabs eat older, inner branches. This creates room among the roots for communities of small creatures. Kelp crabs are vegetarians in the spring, grazing on overgrown water plants and leaving stubby fields behind. In winter, they become carnivores, eating small shellfish and sea slugs.

Occasionally, barnacle parasites latch onto the shells of kelp crabs, penetrating the crab’s shell with an antenna. The parasite then sheds most of its own body parts and slides through the hollow antenna into the crab’s body, where it feeds off its host and breeds offspring.

Strawberry Anemone

With their poisonous stingers, these plantlike animals—less than an inch tall—can eat sea stars, swallowing their victims whole. The stingers carry tiny darts that explode on contact, stunning their prey. Small painted greenling fish are immune to the stingers, and use the anemone as shelter from larger predators. Anemones prefer warm water, and their recent increase in Monterey Bay is considered evidence of global warming by some scientists.

Kelp Forest Surface

Welcome aboard! Before we go below, a reminder: Stay out of the kelp forest itself. Our submersible could damage the plants—and they wouldn’t be too great for our propellers, either!

As we look toward the Monterey shore, notice how calm the water is. That’s because the tops of the kelp plants are keeping wave action to a minimum. Sea otters and harbor seals like to swim through here and snack on snails and crabs that live on the plants. They also wrap themselves up in kelp fronds and nap in them like floating lounge chairs. Anchored to the bottom, the fronds keep the animals from being washed ashore by waves.

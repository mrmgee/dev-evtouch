The Forest Floor

In spring, after winter storms have stripped the forest’s canopy of its thick cover, sunlight streams to the forest floor and nurtures the kelp spores, which give rise to new plants. They attach themselves to the rocky sea bottom at points called holdfasts—and begin their long climb to the surface.

Throughout the summer months, sea stars (starfish) patrol the forest floor, eating bottom-dwelling mollusks. That’s their main diet until the rough seas of fall arrive and shake small shellfish from their perches in the forest’s upper stories. Winter storms yank kelp from their anchors, creating vacancies for next spring’s newcomers. The cycle of life in the forest continues.

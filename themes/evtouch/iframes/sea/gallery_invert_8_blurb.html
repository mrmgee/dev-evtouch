Sun Star

Up to two and a half feet (nearly a meter) across, this carnivorous sea star eats small snails. It lives in the benthic, or bottom, region of the bay, at depths of nearly a mile (1.6 kilometers). The sun star’s world is a dark, cold place where water temperatures rarely top 60 degrees Fahrenheit (5 degrees Celsius) and the pressure is 320 times greater than at the surface.

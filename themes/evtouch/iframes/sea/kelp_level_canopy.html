Kelp Forest Canopy

Here in the kelp forest canopy, just below the surface, the plants get lots of sunlight, and photosynthesis makes their fronds grow fast and thick. For these first four feet (one meter) or so the forest is nearly impenetrable to larger animals, but between the plants squeeze tiny snails, kelp crabs, and small fish. When seasonal storms punish the ocean surface, the canopy can be ripped to shreds and thinned out to virtually nothing. But since giant kelp can grow up to 18 inches (46 centimeters) a day, it doesn’t take long for the forest to grow back to normal.
